# Liquid Chrome — voice guide

The writing target, calibrated from the author's own prose (see the calibration
samples below). The voice is **vivid, visceral, present, and worn** — you are *in*
the body on the wire, living it now, in a voice that's plain-spoken, fatalist,
and quietly furious. Cut filler, never texture. Spare where it snaps; rich where
it counts. Everything else is rewritten to sit next to the samples without
flinching.

## Principles

1. **Present tense, second person, contracted.** "It's the dead of night and you
   hear your console beep." Living it now, spoken plainly — *didn't, you're,
   don't* — never formal, never *did not*.
2. **Comma-spliced momentum, then a blade.** Pile clauses with commas to build a
   breathless run — "a message routes through a dozen encrypted addresses, no name
   on it but one: Kiros" — then stop hard on a short sentence: "Then nothing but an
   empty blinking console." "They didn't kill you." The splice is a feature; it
   controls the breath. Vary the length on purpose.
3. **Concrete tech-horror, named and tactile.** Ground dread in the body and the
   hardware — the liquid chrome in your nerves burned to scar, your body dumped in
   the slums, adhesive peeling with hair and skin, liquid chrome flooding the back
   of the throat. Specific nouns and real damage beat abstract mood.
4. **The em-dash cruelty / the twist.** Hinge the turn on a dash — "and it got you
   burned." "holding your life out like bait — for a while, and for a price."
5. **Worn, fatalist, self-aware.** The narrator is tired and knows the score.
   "start the beginning of what will probably be a long night." "some cold part of
   you notes how little it cost you." Dark humor, resignation, no self-pity.
6. **The ache of the thing out of reach.** Let the wanting show. "the net still out
   there, humming, and you're still in here, hands shaking, with nothing."
7. **Threats have appetite; dialogue is controlled.** The ICE is black and patient,
   you can sense its glee — make menaces *characters*, not weather. People speak
   plainly and a little dangerously; nobody monologues. "You don't know me but I
   know you. Don't keep me waiting."
8. **Quotable closes.** End a beat on a line that shuts like a door. "So we fight
   for every inch of earth we have." "for a while, and for a price."
9. **Clarity underneath the heat.** However vivid, the player always knows what
   happened and what to do. Mechanics stated plainly where they matter (nerve cost,
   credits). Spare is good; cryptic is not.
10. **Retire the dead tics.** Drop crutches that recur without earning it — *the
   chair takes its price*, *grey months*, *the grid past the glass*, *the wet dark*.
   Reach for a fresh, specific image instead.

**Vocabulary is fair game.** ICE, jack in, the wire, the grid, liquid chrome,
credits, the trace, black-site, the slums, the deck — the world's own words, used
with conviction.

## Calibration samples (the author's hand)

**The setting — the cold open.**

> The year is 2055. The corporations rule everything now — every acre, every
> signal, every breath with a price on it. Space travel is a memory, a door that
> closed a generation ago. Every instrument ever pointed at the sky agrees on one
> cold verdict: there is no one else out there. We are alone in all that dark. So
> we fight, corp against corp, for every last inch of earth we have.

**The title hook — the burn.**

> You were the best at what you did — and it got you burned. They didn't kill you.
> They put a toxin in the loop that burned the liquid chrome in your nerves to scar
> tissue, then dumped your body in the slums to rot, the net bright and forever out
> of reach. Then a voice with no name reached back across the dead grid, holding
> your life out like bait — for a while, and for a price.

**The summons — Kiros makes contact.**

> It's the dead of night, and you hear your console beep. A message routes in
> through a dozen encrypted addresses, no name on it but one: Kiros.
>
> "Hello [name]. I'm Kiros. You don't know me, but I know you — everything they
> did, but most importantly, everything you did. I have an offer for you. Come and
> meet me at the old market, in the deep end of the slums. Don't keep me waiting."
>
> Then nothing. An empty console, blinking.
>
> You turn it over for a long minute in the dark of the room. A promise too big to
> be real. But the net is still out there, humming, and you're still in here, hands
> shaking, with nothing. You reach for your coat and start what will probably be a
> long night.

**The trace — a blown run (north star for peril).**

> Before you even open your eyes, you feel it. The trace bites down right as you're
> clawing your way out of the wire, a pressure crawling up the connection, a small
> touch is death. When your eyes finally crack open to a blur of ceiling and dead
> monitor glow, you don't wait for the world to sharpen. You go straight for the
> electrodes, ripping them off your skull in fistfuls, adhesive peeling away with
> hair and skin, liquid chrome taste flooding the back of your throat. Because that
> thing is still coming. The ICE, black and patient, threading itself through the
> open jacks like a parasite that's found a warm body, its next host. You can sense
> its glee as it closes in.
>
> You get the last one off and sit there shaking, electronics around you still
> humming, sweat gone cold down your spine. Doesn't matter. It almost touched you.
> But somehow in that half second before you tore free, it got a taste — your
> signature, the raw shape of what you are when you're naked in the grid. It knows
> you now. That thought alone kills the run dead. You're done for tonight. Maybe
> longer.
