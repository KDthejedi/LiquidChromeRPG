# LIQUID CHROME — World Bible
*Cyberpunk Chronicles, Volume I. The portable canon: setting, characters, voice,
systems, and art direction, consolidated for the isometric-RPG project. This
document is self-contained — a new project needs nothing else to know this world.
Companion docs if available: WRITING.md (voice), STORY.md (arc spec), RPG_AUDIT.md
(approved migration decisions, summarized in §12).*

---

## 1. The world

**The cold open (canon, player-facing):**

> The year is 2055. The corporations rule everything now — every acre, every
> signal, every breath with a price on it. Space travel is a memory, a door that
> closed a generation ago. Every instrument ever pointed at the sky agrees on one
> cold verdict: there is no one else out there. We are alone in all that dark. So
> we fight, corp against corp, for every last inch of earth we have.

That earth is carved up between the South China Sea consortia, the Japanese
technology houses, American defense, Russian cyber-intelligence, the African
intelligence and resource corps, and the Indian, South American and Caribbean
holding companies buying up tech, resources and transit.

**The premise (the player's burn):**

> You were the best at what you did — and it got you burned. They didn't kill
> you. They put a toxin in the loop that burned the liquid chrome in your nerves
> to scar tissue, then dumped your body in the slums to rot, the net bright and
> forever out of reach. Then a voice with no name reached back across the dead
> grid, holding your life out like bait — for a while, and for a price.

**Metaphysics (approved for the RPG — decision D3):** the world is strictly
technological *on the surface*, and "we are alone in the universe" stays true.
The awakened element is **the Hum** — something that woke inside the wire
itself, not out among the stars. Awakened talent is hearing and channeling the
Hum. The governing attribute is **Signal**. Chrome installed in the body
drowns Signal in **Static**: every piece of chrome adds Static, and enough of
it silences the Hum for good. This is the build-defining tradeoff — machine
power versus the wire's own voice. Whatever answers when you channel isn't
from out there. It's from in here.

## 2. The voice

Full guide in WRITING.md. The register is **vivid, visceral, present, worn**:

1. Present tense, second person, contracted — *didn't, you're, don't*. Never formal.
2. Comma-spliced momentum, then a blade: pile clauses, then stop hard. "They didn't kill you."
3. Concrete tech-horror, named and tactile — liquid chrome burned to scar, adhesive peeling with hair and skin.
4. The em-dash cruelty/twist: "holding your life out like bait — for a while, and for a price."
5. Worn, fatalist, self-aware: "Three blocks. How bad can three blocks be."
6. The ache of the thing out of reach: "the net is still out there, humming, and you're still in here, hands shaking, with nothing."
7. Threats have appetite; dialogue is controlled and a little menacing. Nobody monologues.
8. Quotable closes — end a beat on a line that shuts like a door.
9. Clarity underneath the heat: the player always knows what happened and what to do.
10. Dead tics retired: *the chair takes its price*, *grey months*, *the grid past the glass*, *the wet dark*.

**North-star sample (the author's own — a blown netrun):**

> Before you even open your eyes, you feel it. The trace bites down right as
> you're clawing your way out of the wire, a pressure crawling up the connection,
> a small touch is death. When your eyes finally crack open to a blur of ceiling
> and dead monitor glow, you don't wait for the world to sharpen. You go straight
> for the electrodes, ripping them off your skull in fistfuls, adhesive peeling
> away with hair and skin, liquid chrome taste flooding the back of your throat.
> Because that thing is still coming. The ICE, black and patient, threading itself
> through the open jacks like a parasite that's found a warm body, its next host.
> You can sense its glee as it closes in.
>
> You get the last one off and sit there shaking, electronics around you still
> humming, sweat gone cold down your spine. Doesn't matter. It almost touched you.
> But somehow in that half second before you tore free, it got a taste — your
> signature, the raw shape of what you are when you're naked in the grid. It knows
> you now. That thought alone kills the run dead. You're done for tonight. Maybe
> longer.

## 3. Lexicon (canon terms)

- **the wire / the grid / the net** — cyberspace. You **jack in**.
- **liquid chrome** — the living conductive medium wired into an operative's
  nerves; what the burn destroys and the rebuild restores.
- **chrome** — (resource) the body's reserve, drawn down by runs and specials,
  topped up at the clinic. (Also, generically: implants/augments.)
- **credits** — money, always spelled out (no symbol).
- **ICE** — hostile countermeasures on the wire; **black-site** grade kills.
- **the trace** — intrusion detection waking up to you; the run's clock.
- **exploits** — packaged zero-days; consumed to crack firewalls.
- **the burn / burned** — nerve-toxined and cast out; a burned operative.
- **flatline** — death, especially on the wire.
- **fixer** — job broker. **the deep end** — the slum district's heart.
- **the Hum / Signal / Static** — see §1 metaphysics (RPG track).

Home is **Keihin**; travel costs days by vehicle. Each answers to a bloc.

## 5. Factions

- **The corp blocs** (§1) — the world's owners.
- **The four burner corps** (each burned one operative; each is a hunt target):
  **Kaisei** (tech house — burned Zen), **BiTechs Biomedical** (med house —
  burned Socrates), **Griot Intelligence** (intel corp — burned Jackal),
  **the Nakamura Directorate** (shadow corp — burned Hemlock).
- **The fixers:** **Julius Deane** — ninety years old behind a face rented by
  the decade; importer, information; starts you easy. **the Finn** — the fence
  you never meet, only cut-outs and drop-boxes; runs it all from a beach
  somewhere in the Caribbean; never lies about the price, lies about everything
  else. **A voice with no name** — the backer above Kiros; credits clear before
  the job is spoken, and the job is only ever the next address.
- **Kiros's family business:** **Prophet** (eldest — reads the wire; sees the
  shape of a job before it happens), **Queen** (runs the money; moves credits
  through a hundred shells, nothing touches her twice), **Rhys** (youngest —
  moves the muscle; the one you send when talking is done).
- **Street gangs** — wired kids with nothing to lose; the worst kind.

## 6. The four operatives

All four were the best at something, were burned by a different power, and are
rebuilt by Kiros. Legacy stat sheet: strength / speed / stealth / tech /
marksmanship (1–10). Each has four signature **chrome specials** (active kit).

### ZEN — Corporate ghost
- **Bio (locked):** The most feared hacker on the net. Corporations searched far and wide for you — but you were always a ghost.
- **Origin:** You wore a Kaisei badge by day and ran the most feared profile on the grid by night — famous, faceless, and theirs without ever knowing it. When they found what you'd lifted, they didn't fire you. They burned the chrome so you could never jack again.
- **Montage:** (1) By day you wore a Kaisei badge and rode the elevator up past men who couldn't have picked your face out of a lineup. (2) By night you were the most feared profile on the grid — faceless, untouchable, theirs without ever knowing it. Then you lifted the one file they'd kill to keep buried. (3) They didn't fire you. They put a toxin in the loop that burned the liquid chrome in your nerves to dead scar, then dumped your body in the slums to rot. (4) Seven months of that. Your hands still twitch for a deck that isn't there, and the grid burns on without you — close enough to see, dead as stone to touch.
- **Kiros's read:** "Kaisei never even learned your face — just your profile, and how to burn it. I know both."
- **Doctor's read:** "Kaisei toxin work. Neat, corporate, cruel — they metered the dose so you'd feel it finish."
- **Stats:** str 4 · spd 6 · stl 8 · tech 9 · marks 3 · start 350 credits. Deck: Kaisei Ghost-7, company issue.
- **Kit:** *Ghost protocol* (passive — their first shot tracks a profile that isn't there; always misses, once/fight) · *Feedback* (14c — tech-scaled damage down their targeting link + their next move dies in the wire) · *Blackout* (12c — crash their optics; accuracy craters all fight) · *Overclock* (18c, once — next two strikes can't miss and bite deep).
- **Hunt:** Kaisei Tower — the arcology core that still owns your burned profile.
- **Epilogues:** *Payout:* You keep a cheap deck in a drawer you never open. The most feared profile on the grid, and it retired before they ever learned your face. · *Flatline:* Somewhere in Kaisei's logs there's a half-second intrusion, source unresolved. It's the closest thing to a headstone a ghost gets. · *Burn Out:* The net still tells stories about the profile that walked into Kaisei's core and turned the lights off from the inside. · *Ghost:* A year later a new profile surfaces on the grid — quieter, better. Kaisei's countermeasures flinch at it. They should.

### SOCRATES — Struck-off surgeon
- **Bio (locked):** Philosopher and child prodigy. For decades you've worked magic with your hands — manipulating liquid chrome and flesh like an artist.
- **Origin:** A surgeon once, struck off for lifting med-tech from the big houses and using it on people who couldn't pay. BiTechs Biomedical sent people to end it, and they showed you mercy — of a kind. The chrome, not the throat.
- **Montage:** (1) You had the best hands in any theatre, and you spent them on the people the big houses had already crossed off the ledger. (2) The chrome you lifted to do it belonged to BiTechs Biomedical. They don't forgive missing inventory. (3) They sent people to close the account — and gave you mercy, of a kind. They burned the chrome out of your nerves instead of your heart, and left you breathing in the runoff. (4) Struck off, burned out, seven months in a body you know far too well to lie to. It shakes now. You know exactly why.
- **Kiros's read:** "You stole from BiTechs to cut for people who couldn't pay, and they called it mercy when they only took your chrome."
- **Doctor's read:** "BiTechs work, this. Clean as a signature. You've stood on my side of the table — so you know exactly what the next part costs."
- **Stats:** str 6 · spd 4 · stl 5 · tech 8 · marks 6 · start 300 credits. Deck: field-medic rig, heavily modified.
- **Kit:** *Trauma reflex* (passive — survive a killing blow, back at quarter health, once/fight) · *Scalpel* (15c — armour-ignoring cut that bleeds them for three of your turns) · *Sedative* (12c — their damage halves all fight) · *Triage* (16c — stitch yourself back +35% mid-fight).
- **Hunt:** the BiTechs Biomedical vault where they signed off on your death.
- **Epilogues:** *Payout:* You open a back-alley clinic under another name and never charge anyone who can't pay. It isn't revenge. Some nights it's better. · *Flatline:* The hands that saved a hundred people the big houses wrote off finally stop moving. BiTechs files it under equipment loss. · *Burn Out:* Every stolen BiTechs patent goes public on your way down. Clinics in ten districts run on your ashes. · *Ghost:* A free clinic opens in the deep end six months later. Nobody ever meets the surgeon. The waiting list never closes.

### JACKAL — Ex-corp military
- **Bio (locked):** Ran espionage for whoever could pay — CEOs had you on speed dial. The ultimate problem solver.
- **Origin:** You led black espionage for Griot Intelligence, good enough to be handed everything. When you caught your own colonels bleeding the corp dry and moved against them, they hung all of it on you — framed, burned, and turned loose as a warning to the next one.
- **Montage:** (1) You ran black espionage for Griot Intelligence — handed the whole board, because you never once blinked. (2) Then you caught your own colonels bleeding the corp dry. So you did the unforgivable thing. You moved on them. (3) They hung every crime on your name, burned the liquid chrome in your nerves to scar, and threw your body in the slums as a warning to the next fool with a conscience. (4) Seven months a framed man, flinching at every door, the grid a country that exiled you and locked the gate.
- **Kiros's read:** "You caught your own colonels bleeding Griot dry, and they hung the whole rot on you. I've read the real file."
- **Doctor's read:** "Military-grade burn, Griot signature. They wanted the next deserter to read the warning right off your body."
- **Stats:** str 8 · spd 6 · stl 5 · tech 5 · marks 9 · start 300 credits. Deck: Griot field deck, service grade.
- **Kit:** *Iron hide* (passive — first two hits of any fight land soft) · *Suppressing fire* (14c — damage + they lose their next turn) · *Breach round* (15c — armour-piercing heavy hit) · *Execute* (16c, once — below 35% health they're finished outright; above it, heavy damage).
- **Hunt:** Griot Intelligence's inner ring, where your own colonels buried the frame.
- **Epilogues:** *Payout:* The colonels who framed you die rich and old, and you let them. You know what the real file says. Most days that has to be enough. · *Flatline:* Griot buries the intrusion with the rest of its rot — one more soldier written off by men who never once blinked. · *Burn Out:* The frame they hung on you burns with the building. Griot spends years denying the fire had a name. · *Ghost:* One morning every colonel who signed the frame wakes to empty accounts and open files. None of them ever says your name aloud again.

### HEMLOCK — Assassin · poisoner
- **Bio (locked):** Deadly and patient — the best infiltrator anyone never saw coming. You blend into the shadows and move like mercury.
- **Origin:** Poison and patience — the finest infiltrator no one ever saw. The last contract was the trap: a room of your own kind with orders to close the account. One of them let you walk and told you to vanish. You didn't. Here you are, defiant as ever.
- **Montage:** (1) Poison and patience — the finest infiltrator no one ever saw come, and no one ever saw leave. (2) The last contract was the trap: a room full of your own kind, and one order between them. Close your account. (3) One of them let you walk and told you to vanish. You didn't. They burned the chrome from your nerves for the insolence and left you in the gutter to think about it. (4) Seven months since, still breathing, still a splinter under the nail of the people who filled that room.
- **Kiros's read:** "A room full of your own kind was sent to close your account, and they still don't know how you walked out. I do."
- **Doctor's read:** "Contact toxin, skin-delivered. Poisoner's work. Whoever burned you has a sense of humour — your trade, turned on you."
- **Stats:** str 5 · spd 8 · stl 9 · tech 5 · marks 8 · start 200 credits. Deck: silent infiltration rig.
- **Kit:** *Knife's edge* (passive — the blow that would end you simply misses, once/fight) · *Mercury* (15c — a cut quicker than the eye, ignores armour, bites to the bone) · *Burn* (12c — one touch cooks the liquid chrome inside them; every trick they own goes dark for the fight) · *Shadow* (18c, once — blink out and bleed into the dark; the next thing thrown at you finds empty air).
- **Hunt:** the Nakamura Directorate — the corp that filled that room with your own kind.
- **Epilogues:** *Payout:* Nakamura keeps a room on retainer for the day you come back. You never do. You let them keep paying for it. · *Flatline:* Nakamura's countermeasure finishes what a room full of assassins couldn't. They call it a rounding error. They still check the shadows. · *Burn Out:* Nakamura burns, and every assassin on their books quietly tears up the contract. Nobody takes work on a name that did that. · *Ghost:* One by one, the assassins from that room stop reporting in. Nakamura never finds a body. Yours, or theirs.

## 7. The NPCs

**KIROS — the handler.** A light-skinned Black man in his twenties, young for
the weight he carries, calm and unreadable, always caught half in the light
(one augmented eye glows in his portrait). He finds the burned, buys them
whole, arms them, and runs them. He is a **handler, not the head** — a glove
fronting for the voice with no name. Whether he's loyal to it, owned by it, or
quietly running his own angle is the thread pulled tight under everything.
Speaks plainly and a little dangerously: "Don't keep me waiting."

**His summons (canon beat, greets the player by handle):**

> It's the dead of night, and you hear your console beep. A message routes in
> through a dozen encrypted addresses, no name on it but one: Kiros.
> "Hello [name]. I'm Kiros. You don't know me, but I know you — everything they
> did, but most importantly, everything you did. [per-operative read, §6] I have
> an offer for you. Come and meet me at the old market, in the deep end of the
> slums. Don't keep me waiting."
> Then nothing. An empty console, blinking.

**The deal (his offer):** a body that works — the chrome back, better than it
was — money, a gun that isn't a relic, and a road to drive it on. In return: a
handful of jobs across the regions, the ones that matter. Clear the debt, and
then — *then* — he helps you go back for the people who did this to you. All
of it. "And you won't be doing it alone. This is a family business... My blood
— and now, for a while, yours."

**THE TECH-DOCTOR.** Underground surgeon in the clinic beneath Keihin; snaps
on gloves, reads bodies like charts, reads each operative's burn on sight
(§6). "Good bones, under all that ruin." His warning is canon: *"Try not to
enjoy it. The ones who enjoy it don't come back to the body."*

## 8. The story arc

1. **The burn** — see §1. Seven months in the slums, hands twitching for a deck that isn't there.
2. **The summons** — Kiros's console message; the walk through the deep end;
   the gang confrontation (**the first moral choice: fists / gun / bribe**,
   feeding the morality ledger — lethal / nonlethal / guile); the old market;
   tea with Kiros; the deal there was never a choice about; the chair.
3. **The debt** — ~16 contracts across ten districts: fixer money-work plus
   Kiros's five story jobs (they only surface when a city's fixer board is
   clear, and run in arc order). Mission shapes: heists, rescues, pursuits,
   netruns; road encounters between cities.
4. **The intel** — each story job pulls a fragment; how *clean* you run grades
   it (clean +3 / rushed +1 / traced 0). The trail: a shell called Meridian
   Holdings → the shell's parents → Kiros is on a leash too → the name that
   lands in your gut like a swallowed stone: your own burner → **the turn**:
   the voice with no name is a hand of the corp that burned you. You've been
   running their errands all along. Kiros was the glove they wore to reach you.
5. **The hunt** — one job left on the board, and you posted it: your burner's
   black-site core. No retainer. No fixer.
6. **Four endings** (+ per-operative epilogues, §6): **The Payout** (walk with
   the money; the wire keeps saying your name through the wall) · **Flatline**
   (their countermeasure closes its hand around your heart) · **Burn Out**
   (take them down and burn inside it) · **Ghost** (take them apart without a
   sound and vanish — requires a well-earned dossier).

## 9. Systems built (mechanics-as-lore reference)

- **Chrome (reserve):** jacking in draws it; specials burn it; the clinic tops
  it up for credits; empty = flatline. The tension: the same pool that powers
  your tricks is the one that gets you out alive.
- **Trace:** the run clock. ≥70% at the pull = rushed; caught = traced.
  Black-site ICE kills on a catch.
- **Trauma stabiliser:** clinic hardware; argues with death and wins exactly
  once, then burns out and must be re-bought (500 credits).
- **Morality ledger:** lethal / nonlethal / guile counters written by choices,
  starting with the gang confrontation.
- **Intel/dossier:** the earned-truth system (§8.4); gates the Ghost ending.
- **Contract loop:** fixer board → brief → prep (clinic/garage) → mission →
  debrief → spend. Leveling: a point every 2 contracts → stat or new special.
- **Vehicles (8):** mule → kabuki → lowboy → katana → onyx → vulon → shinden →
  seraph (speed = travel days). The top four carry **weapons** usable on road
  ambushes for a per-use ammo cost: Onyx *Limpet spinners* (150), Vulon
  *Shredder pod* (120), Shinden *Flechette turret* (180), Seraph *Lock-on arc
  missiles* (250 — everything behind you dies at once: engines, weapons, lights).
- **Netrun lattice:** procedural node columns (entry → core), ICE to crack
  with exploits or brute-force, data nodes with loose credits, the trace
  filling behind you. **This is the RPG's intrusion minigame, already built.**

## 12. RPG evolution — approved decisions (from RPG_AUDIT.md)

- **D1** Evolve in place; the game stays playable at every merge.
- **D2** Attributes: **Body** (←strength) · **Reflex** (←speed) · **Mind**
  (←tech) · **Presence** (new) · **Signal** (new). Old stealth/marksmanship
  become skills (Infiltration, Firearms) in a ~11-skill sheet (Firearms, Close
  Combat, Infiltration, Electronics, Intrusion, Negotiation, Intimidation,
  Streetwise, Perception, Channeling, Medicine).
- **D3** Metaphysics: **the Hum / Signal / Static** (§1). Of the wire, not the
  stars.
- **D4** AI narrative: authored-first, always-working fallbacks; AI is texture
  (ambient lines, combat-log variants) inside WRITING.md guardrails; plot
  always authored. Static hosting = no server keys; user-supplied key only.
- **D5** Isometric presentation: walkable code-drawn canvas scenes — neon
  wireframe floors/walls in the house palette, actors as portrait-chips with
  glow rings, tap-to-move — explore, talk, and fight turn-based in the same
  view. No tile or character sprites (keeps art code-driven; sidesteps
  AI-sprite consistency).
- **Phases:** P1 skeleton (sheet, saves, iso safehouse) → P2 loop (creation,
  fixer talk, one 2-approach contract) → P3 combat → P4 depth (intrusion,
  Hum/Static, factions) → P5 polish. Gate at each phase end.
