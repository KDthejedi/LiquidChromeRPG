# Liquid Chrome — story

*Cyberpunk Chronicles, Volume I. The canonical narrative. Register: the
north-star voice in WRITING.md — vivid, visceral, present. In the body on the
wire, menace in the tech, the world's own words used with conviction.*

## The world

The year is 2055. The corporations rule everything now — every acre, every
signal, every breath with a price on it. Space travel is a memory, a door that
closed a generation ago. Every instrument ever pointed at the sky agrees on one
cold verdict: there is no one else out there. We are alone in all that dark. So
we fight, corp against corp, for every last inch of earth we have.

That earth is carved up between the South China Sea consortia, the Japanese
technology houses, American defense, Russian cyber-intelligence, the African
intelligence and resource corps, and the Indian, South American and Caribbean
holding companies buying up tech, resources and transit.

## The burn

You were a console jacker once — the best in the world. Everyone knew your
profile; no one ever put a face to it. Then you stole from the wrong people —
corrupt, already bleeding everyone else dry, so why not — and they didn't kill
you. They did worse. They slid a toxin into your nervous system that cooks nerve
down to scar, so you could never jack in again, and turned you loose in the flesh
to rot in sight of the wire you could no longer touch. Seven months of that, your
hands twitching for a deck that wasn't there.

## The offer

Then **Kiros** reaches across the dead grid — a young man who deals only from the
shadows, half-lit and unreadable. Money you never earned is already sitting in a
cyber-hardened account that isn't yours; under the coordinates, one word:
**soon**. He buys your surgery in an underground clinic strung with more chrome
than a corp ward and hands you a second life: new nerve, a fresh arsenal of tech
and weapons, and the old absence where the wire used to be, suddenly and terribly
filled. Then he hands you a string of runs. He isn't the top of this — Kiros is a
handler, a glove fronting for a voice with no name, a power above him you never
meet. *"Try not to enjoy it,"* the surgeon says, snapping off the gloves. *"The
ones who enjoy it don't come back to the body."*

## The work

You take jobs off a board shared by a fixed cast of fixers:

- **Julius Deane** — the ninety-year-old importer behind a rented synthetic
  face. Starts you easy: a cold-storage node, then a Maas Biolabs records annex.
- **the Finn** — the fence. You never meet him, only his cut-outs and drop-boxes
  of dead tech; word is he runs the whole thing from a beach somewhere in the
  Caribbean. Feeds you side work for money and nerve-risk.
- **a voice with no name** — the power above your handler Kiros. Posts the jobs
  that matter; the deeper you get, the clearer it is Kiros is only the messenger.

Each run bleeds a little more off the debt. Do what they want, and keep your head
down doing it. And then, when the debt is paid and the promises come due — **you
go back for the ones who burned you in the first place.**

## Intel — earned by how well you run

Every run releases a little more of the truth — but **how much, and how clean,
depends on how well you pull it off.** You're not just being paid; you're
quietly reading the people paying you. The better the run, the sharper the
fragment you carry out:

- **Clean** (data core pulled, trace kept low, the extra data nodes grabbed, no
  firewall brute-forced) → a clear, complete intel fragment. You learn something real.
- **Rushed** (pulled it, but the trace ran hot or you brute-forced a firewall and
  screamed your address) → a partial, hedged fragment. Enough to suspect, not to be sure.
- **Traced** (caught, run lost) → scrap: garbled, corrupted, or nothing — and
  sometimes a deliberate lie planted for anyone sloppy enough to get burned.

Those fragments accumulate into a dossier on the nameless backer and, slowly,
on the org that burned you. **What you know at the end is exactly what you
earned run by run** — and it shapes the hunt: how much of the reveal you get,
how prepared you walk in, and which endings are within reach.

## The four operatives

You play one of four — the burned Kiros rebuilds. Each was the best at something
and was burned by a different power; the surgery restored the nerve and added an
arsenal, so all of them jack.

1. **Zen — corporate ghost.** Ex-corporate out of a Japanese tech house
   (**Kaisei**): behind a desk by day, the most feared hacker profile on the grid
   by night. They found what you stole and burned you.
2. **Socrates — struck-off surgeon.** Debarred for stealing med-tech from the big
   houses and using it to treat the poor. Those companies (**BiTechs
   Biomedical**) sent killers. They showed you mercy — of a kind.
3. **Jackal — ex-corp military.** Ran corp espionage for an African
   intelligence corp (**Griot Intelligence**); a hand-to-hand and military
   expert. You found your superiors scamming the corp and fought back, got
   framed for all of it, and were burned and turned loose as a warning.
4. **Hemlock — assassin & poisoner.** Infiltration is your art. You did one last
   job for an unknown corp and found yourself surrounded by other assassins with
   orders to end you. One of them showed you mercy and warned you: get out of the
   game, never show your face. But here you are, defiant as ever.

## Kiros — the benefactor

The man in the shadows who hands the four their second life: a light-skinned
Black man in his twenties, young for the weight he carries, calm and unreadable,
always caught half in the light. He finds the burned, buys them whole, arms them,
and runs them. But Kiros is a **handler, not the head** — a glove fronting for the
voice with no name, a deeper power he answers to. Whether he's loyal to it, owned
by it, or quietly running his own angle underneath is the thread pulled tight
under everything.

## The endgame

**The debt (ten runs).** The fixers' board carries roughly ten jobs. You work
them to clear the debt — Deane's early nodes, the Finn's side work, and the
nameless voice's dangerous contracts. Each one pays down what you owe and pushes
your nerve.

**The turn.** With the debt paid, the board changes. The voice with no name —
the power above Kiros — is revealed to be **tied to the very corp/org that burned
you**. You have been working, all this time and through Kiros, for a hand of the
thing that took your life. Which leaves Kiros: was he in on it, used like you, or
running his own game? *How completely the tie comes clear, and how much you know
about your target walking in, is exactly the intel you earned across the ten
runs.* And now the one job left on the board is your own: **the hunt.** You go
after the ones who burned you.

**Your hunt is personal — and it changes with who you are:**

| Operative | The hunt |
|---|---|
| Zen | **Kaisei** — the tech house that owned your profile and burned it |
| Socrates | **BiTechs Biomedical** — the house that hunted you for healing the poor |
| Jackal | **Griot Intelligence** — the corp that framed you to bury its own rot |
| Hemlock | **the unknown corp** — the one the reveal finally names |

**The four endings** (chosen by how the hunt goes, how much intel you earned,
and what you decide at the end — the cleanest exit, *vanish*, takes a
well-earned dossier to reach):

1. **Walk with the money** — take the payout and leave the trigger unpulled. The
   debt's clear and you live, but the ones who burned you go on standing — and the
   wire keeps saying your name through the wall.
2. **Flatline** — the hunt's black-site countermeasure closes its hand around your
   heart. You die on the wire, mid-reach, taking your shot.
3. **Burn out** — you bring them down, and it costs everything: a blaze that takes
   you with it. They're gone, and so are you, a name the strip says for a week.
4. **Vanish** — you take the revenge without a sound and disappear into the
   sprawl, a ghost the corps never manage to account for.
